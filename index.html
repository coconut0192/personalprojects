Current Limitations & Learnings

Note: OpenClaw is constantly getting updates, so it's highly likely many of these constraints have been fixed by now.

  • Revealed the absolute limitations of single-shot prompts, proving the necessity of chained multi-agent workflows for complex tasks.
  • Browser automation operations kept failing or timing out during complex interactions.
  • Smaller AI models mess up even simple tasks and is not consistent with the response.
  • Inconsistent API limits of the high quality AI models make it difficult to have a project be actually used on a day to day basis.
  • Live scraping APIs for job postings proved inconsistent; manual data entry provided significantly better results for skill analysis.

Guiding Systems That Act

We’re moving toward systems that improve with less intervention—where the role shifts from prompting to oversight.

With frameworks like OpenClaw, Gemini CLI, Claude Code, and Perplexity Computer, access is expanding—from standard chat prompts to full system interaction. The barrier between natural language and local operating system control is rapidly dissolving.

As these systems move toward greater autonomy, I’m focused on learning how to work alongside them—evaluating outputs, managing behavior, and building workflows that hold up in practice.

The goal is to get better at guiding and orchestrating systems that are becoming more capable—requiring less direct input, but demanding more responsibility in how they’re used.
